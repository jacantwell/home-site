The decision

Scenario A — Accept our £4,600 offer: We move in in 15 days. Landlord receives £4,600 × 12 = £55,200 over the year.

Scenario B — Hold out for £4,750: Property sits empty until a new tenant is found, then £4,750 × 12 = £57,000. The gain is only £1,800/year (3.3%) — assuming they actually achieve the £4,750 ask.

Assumptions and caveats

This analysis is conservative — actually favourable to the landlord. The break-even number ignores costs that work against Scenario B:
  • Void costs: council tax, utilities, insurance all paid by the landlord while empty (~£300–£500/month combined).
  • Re-letting fees: agent fees, marketing, referencing — typically 1 month's rent (£4,750) or a 5–10% fee.
  • Tenant risk: we are confirmed, referenced and ready. A replacement tenant is unknown.
  • £4,750 may not be achieved. If the landlord eventually drops to £4,500 (like Hollydale), the entire premium evaporates.
Once these costs are included, the real break-even falls to ~3-5 days of additional vacancy.
