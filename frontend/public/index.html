The asking-vs-clearing gap

Current asking prices for 5-bed houses (orange) plotted against the official ONS rental distribution for 4+ bed properties in SE15 (blue band)
The gap is the story. Even the lowest current asking price (£4,500) sits above the SE15 4+ bed upper quartile (£3,600). The £4,750 cluster is 44% above the SE15 median. Some of that gap reflects 5-bed-specific premium over the 4+ bed average — but not all of it.

Asking price vs days on market

Negotiation talking points

  1. Cite the ONS data directly. "The official ONS rental data for SE15 shows the median 4+ bed rent at £3,300 and the upper quartile at £3,600. Even allowing for 5-bed specifics, £4,500 is already above market."
  2. Three near-identical OpenRent properties (Kincaid / Crewys / Dayton Grove) all asking £4,750 — none have let in 7 days. Same landlord, identical wording. Clear evidence £4,750 is aspirational, not clearing.
  3. Hollydale Road has been at £4,500 for 60 days with no reduction. Confirms the £4,500 band exists but is sticky — landlords are testing, tenants are not biting.
  4. Use the Lanvanor/Fenham/Nutcroft inconsistency. Same agent (Gareth James) prices Lanvanor (1,065 sq ft) at £5,500 but Nutcroft (1,260 sq ft) at £5,000. They're not pricing on fundamentals.
  5. Use the Camberwell SE5 let-agreed comparator. A Victorian 5-bed asked £4,900 and took ~8 weeks to let. Anything sub-Camberwell quality should price below that.
